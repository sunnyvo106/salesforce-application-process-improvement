The organisation uses Salesforce to manage its application and recruitment process. While the system supports core operational needs, a number of process and system limitations have reduced efficiency, clarity, and data quality over time.

These challenges became particularly evident during onboarding, day-to-day recruitment operations, and system maintenance, highlighting the need for a structured review and optimisation of the existing process.

### 1. Lack of Process Documentation

### 2. Manual and Inconsistent Applicant Follow-Ups
Applicant communication relied on manual checks and ad-hoc follow-ups. The absence of structured workflows or tracking mechanisms resulted in delayed responses, inconsistent communication, and reduced visibility over application progress.

---

### 3. Salesforce System Constraints

### 4. Redundant and Conflicting Workflow Logic

---

### 5. Data Quality and Governance Risks
Unused fields, unclear ownership of data elements, and inconsistent updates reduced confidence in reporting accuracy and made system maintenance more complex.
